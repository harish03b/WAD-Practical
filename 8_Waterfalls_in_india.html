Discover the breathtaking beauty of India's natural wonders

Famous Waterfalls

Jog Falls

Shimoga, Karnataka

Jog Falls is the second-highest plunge waterfall in India. Located on the Sharavathi River, it drops 253 meters (830 ft) in four distinct cascades known as Raja, Rani, Rover, and Rocket.

Dudhsagar Falls

Goa-Karnataka border

Dudhsagar Falls is a four-tiered waterfall on the Mandovi River with a total height of 310 meters (1017 ft). The falls are named for their appearance of milk streaming down the mountain.

Athirappilly Falls

Thrissur, Kerala

Known as the "Niagara of India," Athirappilly Falls is the largest waterfall in Kerala, dropping 25 meters (82 ft). The falls are located on the Chalakudy River in the Sholayar ranges.

Nohkalikai Falls

Cherrapunji, Meghalaya

Nohkalikai Falls is the tallest plunge waterfall in India, dropping 340 meters (1115 ft). The falls are fed by rainwater collected on the summit of the plateau and are most spectacular during monsoon.

Kunchikal Falls

Shimoga, Karnataka

Kunchikal Falls is the highest waterfall in India with a total height of 455 meters (1493 ft). Located on the Varahi River, these falls are part of a hydroelectric project and access is restricted.

Barehipani Falls

Mayurbhanj, Odisha

Barehipani Falls is the second highest waterfall in India with a total height of 399 meters (1309 ft). Located in Simlipal National Park, it's a two-tiered waterfall on the Budhabalanga River.

How to Reach Waterfalls in India

By Air

Most major waterfalls are accessible via nearby airports. For example:

  • Jog Falls: Hubli Airport (100 km away)
  • Dudhsagar Falls: Dabolim Airport, Goa (60 km away)
  • Athirappilly Falls: Cochin International Airport (55 km away)

By Train

Indian Railways provides good connectivity to many waterfall locations:

  • Jog Falls: Nearest station is Talguppa (10 km away)
  • Dudhsagar Falls: Nearest station is Castle Rock (14 km away)
  • Nohkalikai Falls: Nearest station is Guwahati (150 km away)

By Road

Most waterfalls are accessible by road, though some may require trekking:

  • Well-maintained roads connect to major waterfalls
  • Local taxis and buses available from nearby towns
  • Some remote falls require 4WD vehicles or hiking
Tip: The best time to visit most waterfalls is during or just after the monsoon season (June-September) when water flow is at its peak.
